10 — Alternative Strategies

What to do when specific parts of the system underperform.

No campaign runs perfectly from day one. The difference between an agency that generates results and one that burns budget is knowing the right response for each failure mode. These are the specific tactical responses for Fantail's most likely scenarios.

Scenario 01
Google clicks not converting to enquiries
Traffic is arriving. Forms are not being filled. CPL is too high relative to click volume.
Conversion problem — not a traffic problem

The instinct is to adjust the Google ads. The real problem is almost always the landing page — CTA not clear enough, price context missing, or the page loads too slowly on mobile. Before touching ad spend, diagnose the page.

Response

Check page load speed first (target 90+ mobile score on Google PageSpeed Insights). Then check: is there a visible phone number and form above the fold? Add price context ("from $X installed"). Install Hotjar heatmaps to see exactly where users drop off. Usually one of these three things resolves it within 2 weeks.

Alternative tactic

Add a click-to-call extension directly in the Google ad — HVAC buyers often prefer calling over filling in a form. A prominent phone number in the ad itself (not just the landing page) can double enquiry rate without touching the page or the keyword list.

Scenario 02
Meta ads generating clicks but no form submissions
High Meta CTR, reasonable spend, but clicks are not turning into enquiries on the page.
Audience or funnel stage mismatch

Meta is a pre-intent channel — it creates awareness, not immediate conversions. If it's measured on the same CPL benchmark as Google, it will always look underperforming. The right response is to adjust the funnel, not the budget.

Response

Switch the Meta objective from website traffic to lead generation forms — capture name and phone directly in the ad without requiring a website visit. HVAC buyers on Meta are in early research; a low-friction "get a free quote" form converts this audience significantly better than directing them to a page.

Alternative tactic

Run a "heat pump health check" offer — free 30-minute inspection for existing owners — rather than a direct install pitch. This removes price friction entirely. Book the inspection, build trust in person during the visit, then upsell the new installation. Lower CPL on Meta, higher close rate because of in-person trust.

Scenario 03
LinkedIn generating impressions but no B2B leads
Ads are running, facility managers are seeing them, but no enquiries after 6–8 weeks.
Offer or targeting mismatch

B2B LinkedIn cycles are long — 6–8 weeks of zero direct enquiries is normal and expected. But if there are zero engagement signals at all (no profile visits, no link clicks, no form opens), that points to a targeting or creative problem, not just a timing problem.

Response

Broaden targeting to include company follower targeting in property management, hospitality, retail, and healthcare — industries with large HVAC needs in Wellington. Switch creative to a specific, concrete offer: "Commercial HVAC maintenance contracts — guaranteed 4-hour response SLA for Wellington businesses." Specificity drives B2B response.

Alternative tactic

Direct LinkedIn outreach — manually connect with facility managers at Wellington companies, send a personalised message referencing a specific pain point for their industry. Organic LinkedIn outreach is slower but zero-cost and produces warmer leads than cold ad impressions in the early months.

Scenario 04
Enquiries arriving but not converting to booked jobs
Marketing is working — leads are coming in — but close rate from enquiry to booked job is low.
Sales process — not a marketing problem

This is the most important scenario to diagnose correctly. Common causes: response time over 2 hours (HVAC buyers shop multiple providers simultaneously and go with whoever responds first), quote turnaround too slow, or price presentation that doesn't justify the value.

Response

Track response time to every enquiry — if it's over 2 hours, close rate will be low regardless of lead quality. Set up an auto-responder on the enquiry form confirming receipt and setting a clear expectation ("We'll call you within 2 hours"). This alone often lifts close rate significantly because it removes uncertainty before the first call.

Marketing assist

Add retargeting for enquiry form abandoners — people who started filling out the form but didn't submit. Follow them with a "Get a free in-home quote — no obligation" Meta ad for 14 days. Separately, a post-enquiry email sends case studies and reviews to warm prospects who are still comparing providers.

Scenario 05
Competitor increases spend and Google CPC rises sharply
A Wellington competitor increases their budget — CPC rises, Fantail's ad position drops, CPL increases.
Competitive pressure — requires diversification

This is why organic and supporting channels matter. A business entirely dependent on paid search is vulnerable to competitor budget increases. The GBP reviews, SEO rankings, email list, and referral programme built through the system provide channels that a competitor's ad spend cannot directly affect.

Immediate response

Shift budget toward long-tail and service-specific keywords competitors are less likely to target — "Mitsubishi heat pump installer Wellington," "Daikin heat pump install Karori," "heat pump install Wellington villa." These convert higher and face less competition than broad terms where the bidding war is happening.

Long-term defence

GBP review count and organic SEO rankings cannot be bought. A Fantail profile with 80+ five-star reviews and a service page ranking organically for "heat pump install Wellington" costs a competitor months and significant budget to approach — these assets are built through the system and compound over time.

The core principle across all of these: Every underperformance scenario has a specific diagnosis and a specific fix. The monthly reporting built into Track 1+2 exists precisely to identify which scenario is active and respond with the right lever — not a generalised budget increase or a pivot to an entirely new channel. This is what separates managed campaigns from set-and-forget advertising.
